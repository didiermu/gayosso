Use Case | 2023

Global Design & Localization

COMPANY

Insulet

INDUSTRY

Medical Devices

DURATION

Ongoing

ROLE PLAYED

UX Design Manager

Generating Consistent User Experiences Across Markets

Introduction

When I joined Insulet as the UX Manager, overseeing the localization of our primary apps, the program was just beginning. To achieve Insulet’s ambitious goals of expanding into international markets, including those with right-to-left languages, I hired and onboarded new UX team members to tackle these challenges. Concurrently, Insulet’s localization process was being restructured, and the emerging cross-functional team explored various strategies to navigate this transition. We iterated on ways to clarify direction, considering critical elements required for scalable translation and localization, cohesive design strategies for Insulet as a global platform, and the complexities of working with partners like Abbott, Dexcom, and Glooko.

Outcome

We are nearing the completion of a second approach that could become a streamlined, robust, and cohesive localization process. With the help of LLM tools, the team is now aligned and equipped to handle the challenges of localization. We have transitioned from initial uncertainty to a position of confidence and creativity, ready to deliver localized versions of our main app efficiently. My role involved providing hands-on support for the strategy, optimizing existing processes, and establishing new ones to ensure brand and content consistency in a scalable manner. Tactically, this included ensuring a successful migration from Sketch to Figma, configuring dynamic content for different markets, and implementing new tools and plugins.

Process

An executive decision to centralize localization within the UX team, although unconventional, proved advantageous. Several decisions emerged as the team prepared to localize two critical revenue-generating apps.

One app was already localized and on the market, with no major updates planned. Introducing additional languages, including right-to-left and logographic/alphabetic-syllabic scripts, added new complexities. On the other hand, the upcoming flagship product was in the midst of defining and refining key features and had not been localized previously. Legacy code further complicated the localization process, despite the simpler initial focus on markets with Latin/Romance languages.

Both scenarios were pivotal in shaping the overarching strategy aimed at establishing a robust foundation. From a user experience standpoint, the UX team identified three key areas that formed the cornerstone of our localization strategy, especially in managing integrations with major industry players such as Abbott, Dexcom, and Glooko:

1. Branding and Content Consistency

Ensuring a cohesive visual design and brand identity across multiple integrations is critical. Each partner has its own design guidelines, which can lead to inconsistencies in the app's appearance and user experience.

A visual content strategy that allows for our partners’ specifications and identity while emphasizing Insulet’s brand, serves as a guiding principle to achieve consistency across all markets. For instance, I am leading an ongoing initiative to develop a unified design system, establishing guidelines for color schemes, typography, iconography, and other visual elements. This system ensures that the app maintains a consistent visual identity across all integrations and markets, supporting a seamless user experience. While our team hasn't achieved complete uniformity across platforms (iOS and Android), I am steering the effort to steadily approach that goal.

Additionally, maintaining Insulet’s brand involved ensuring consistency in terminology and cultural and medical appropriateness, achieved through the development of a comprehensive glossary of terms. This glossary standardized translations across all languages and regions, encompassing industry-specific terminology, brand-specific terms, and commonly used product-related phrases. Its implementation ensured translation consistency, reduced ambiguities, and preserved the brand’s voice across different languages. Moreover, it expedited the translation process by providing a definitive reference for translators. Regular updates and proper utilization of this glossary were crucial, empowering the responsible team to resolve conflicts effectively.

2. Adoption of a Global Design Mindset

Having a global design mindset may sound simple. One might think that generating responsive specs and following accessibility practices is sufficient. For the UX team at Insulet, this was a good start, but we needed to review our responsive specifications for the product in the maturity/maintenance phase, as these specifications did not originally account for languages that expanded greatly or had different formats. In this context, one of my teams focused solely on redesigning UI layouts to adapt to different text lengths and ensuring all UI elements were tested for responsiveness.

For the product in the expansion phase, we had to redesign some layouts, but we were fortunate that some features were still under development. The strategy here was to create designs that prevented UI issues arising from text expansion or contraction in different languages. This ensures a uniform user experience across all localized versions of the app (on Android and iOS), maintaining usability and aesthetic integrity. We aim to create an experience for all markets that is at least as good as the one for the US. I am proud to champion consistency across markets, ensuring a standard experience regardless of country or language.

As the team and the process matured, I co-led the UX team to focus on designing a global product for users with diverse cultural backgrounds but the same goal: to have the best quality, safe, and simple insulin delivery. Having fully flexible and responsive mockups was just the beginning of our product's expansion as the market and ecosystem grew (and continues to grow). This is particularly important for the universe of devices on the Android platform, including the recent foldable devices, and to support right-to-left languages on both Android and iOS.

In parallel, as we migrated from Sketch to Figma, I asked the team to rebuild our components and screens to be fully responsive. This was a bold decision at the time as the design team was not yet aligned, but I’m glad I made it with the support of my peers and manager. It allowed us to reuse these screens in other supporting products, positioning us well for the future.

3. Dynamic Content Management

The last and most challenging piece was building the infrastructure to share the same strings between the code build and the UX files.

We have only just begun, and the scope currently includes only the product in the expansion phase. I can see why this might not have been feasible three years ago. But now, with the available technology, we are leveraging it.

With strong leadership from our director, we established a rigorous process to align and share the same copy across our UX documents and the code. This streamlined the translation process, eliminating the need to wait for the build to be ready. The initial effort to audit and ensure all text elements (strings) within the apps were perfectly aligned was intense. My team needed to check line by line, pairing the copy and reviewing discrepancies, while mimicking how the development team built the code. This involved detailed reviews, context explanations, and collaboration with different teams to ensure accuracy.

This is still a work in progress, but perfect alignment on strings is facilitating smoother integration of translations into the app. Although we are not finished, as our ecosystem is complex with various platforms, versions, and supporting products, we can already see the benefits of this hard work.

Conclusion:

The journey from forming a UX Design team with little to no knowledge of translation and localization to executing a high-speed localization strategy has been transformative. By focusing on creating a comprehensive glossary, designing with a global mindset, developing detailed responsive specs, and ensuring perfect alignment of strings, my team has built a solid foundation for our global expansion. Reflecting on the extended cross-functional team capabilities, our ability to collectively adapt and lead through uncertainty has positioned us to enter new markets swiftly and confidently. I am proud to be part of this journey.
