The Unlikely Mentorship

The Feedback Hunt
Undeterred by my boss’s uninspiring response.

Ah, the good times of being the last pick on your boss’s team –that feeling of missing the train for a fraction of a second... Imagine this: you're putting in the hours, hoping for a crumb of feedback from your boss, only to get those not-so-subtle eye rolls when you dare to ask. It's like trying to get advice from a grumpy cat – futile and a bit soul-crushing. But hey, fear not, fellow adventurers! Come along as I share the hilarious tale of hunting down mentorship and sprucing up my vibe among strangers.

The Unlikely Hunt for Executive Swagger


I discovered that enhancing my performance and mastering executive presence, or at least trying to, isn't a paint-by-numbers job or something you learn from a self-help book. But when asking your manager for general feedback gets you rehearsed jargon, and even raised eyebrows -and maybe a chuckle or two- when asking how to build that executive presence, it is time to look somewhere else. And don’t get me wrong, I understand in fast-growing companies there is very limited time, and yet having this input is indispensable for personal and professional development.

A Comedy of Missteps: The Feedback Hunt


Undeterred by my boss’s uninspiring response, I embarked on a mentorship quest. First stop: the land of ex-colleagues, where peer advice flowed like the coffee. Sure, some were there only for the caffeine fix, but beggars can’t be choosers, right? Yet, amidst the chatter, I had some enlightening talks. And here’s the kicker – you don’t need weekly therapy-like sessions; a single chat can be a beacon of brilliance.

Meanwhile, I braved the choppy waters of workplace feedback, stumbling upon pals willing to drop truth bombs without the sugar coating. True friendship, it turns out, is honesty with a sprinkle of kindness. I cannot express sufficient gratitude to those individuals who have enlightened my way.

Then, by some stroke of luck, I stumbled upon the underrated world of group and peer mentoring, where anonymity ruled. It wasn’t a secret society of workplace misfits, but a gang of strangers united on Zoom calls to fine-tune their skills and executive vibes. In their anonymity lay the key to unlocking the secrets of presence. No judgments, no expectations – just a raw, honest mirror to reflect and grow.

From old work buddies (past), current allies (present), to potential pals (future), each encounter was a lesson in the art of presence – a chance to dance the communication tango and leave a lasting impression.

Key Takeaways

  • Be proactive: When mentorship is nowhere to be found, seize control. Embrace the chaos and seek out feedback.
  • Mix it up: Look beyond your office circle for wisdom, whether it's ex-colleagues, or even random group mentoring sessions.
  • Roll with the punches: Not all feedback is gold. Learn to sift through the criticism and keep moving forward.
  • Embrace the weirdness: Bond with fellow strangers. Sometimes, the best advice comes from the most unexpected sources.

Take charge of your career journey, folks, and if you need a stranger to test your executive stand-up routine on, you know where to find me!
