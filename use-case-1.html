Use Case | 2023

Sketch to Figma Migration

COMPANY

Insulet

INDUSTRY

Medical Devices

DURATION

12 Months

ROLE PLAYED

UX Design Manager

Navigating Tool Evolution with AWS Criteria

Introduction

In the dynamic realm of UX Design, where technology continuously reshapes our methodologies, the decision to transition from Sketch to Figma stood out as pivotal. This shift was more than just choosing a new tool; it involved strategically aligning our business goals across all markets with innovative solutions, recognizing the transformative potential of our decision.

Outcome

Our journey involved a complete reimagining of our design system, with a focus on cleaning up the legacy componentry we faced. Not apparent at the beginning, we later realized that we were adhering to AWS's 7 Rs framework - Assess, Prioritize, Refactor, Retire, Retain, Revisit, and Validate - as we navigated through the transition. The result enabled a streamlined localization process, aligned with Insulet's time-to-market goals and business strategies. Achieving this meant refactoring over 2000 screens, adding identifiers to each string field, organizing tailored training sessions, and ensuring the seamless adoption and accessibility of our deliverables for stakeholders, designers, developers, and testers alike.

Process

Leading the transition from Sketch to Figma for a product entrenched in legacy components, presented both challenges and opportunities for our dedicated creative team. Although adherence to AWS's 7 Rs framework wasn't initially clear, our approach encompassed the following stages:

Assessment, Prioritization, and Path Determination:

We meticulously evaluated the potential value for the company, estimating the scope of work, and identifying associated costs and risks. Through this thorough evaluation, we charted a path forward, ensuring continuity of work amidst the migration. Additionally, recognizing the unfamiliar terrain of this transition, we allowed space for an iterative approach, acknowledging the need for flexibility in navigating this new territory.

Refactoring:

The overhaul of our design system demanded a strategic approach. We struggled with the dilemma of needing to maintain existing variations versus simplifying and unifying elements. With simplification as our guiding principle, we meticulously selected the patterns to preserve, maintaining essential components while paving the way for future enhancements. We placed significant emphasis on responsiveness to ensure consistency across devices and to prepare our product for the fiercely competitive landscape of medical device design.

Retirement:

Maintaining two systems concurrently is arduous and time-consuming, but it was necessary. This decision, to retain both systems while gradually phasing out Sketch, was motivated not only by cost considerations but also by the imperative to mitigate risks associated with regulatory compliance.

Retention and Revisitation:

Critical tools, such as our flow generation tool, were retained to preserve workflow efficiency. Recognizing the time-consuming nature of rebuilding all flows, we strategically prioritized retention and sought ways to minimize manual migration efforts.

Validation:

Quality assurance played a pivotal role in our migration, with the testing team ensuring thorough validation of the migrated assets, covering both visual fidelity and content accuracy. This meticulous, though time-consuming, validation was essential to maintaining the integrity of our UX assets. Navigating the migration amidst ongoing work presented unforeseen challenges and hurdles. However, through strategic planning and strong leadership, we kept the migration on track while ensuring continued productivity.

Conclusion:

While outsourcing this effort was an option, keeping it in-house empowered us to pivot and expand scope as needed. Our approach not only ensured a comprehensive transition but also laid the groundwork for future automation and integration. As UX Manager and co-lead, I realized this initiative highlighted the collaborative potential within our teams, reinforcing the significance of adaptable design methodologies supported by robust systems. This strategic move positioned our product team for continued adaptation to evolving design paradigms.
